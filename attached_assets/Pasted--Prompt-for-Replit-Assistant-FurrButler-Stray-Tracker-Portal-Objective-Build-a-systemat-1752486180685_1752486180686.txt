### 🐾 Prompt for Replit Assistant: FurrButler Stray Tracker Portal

**Objective:**
Build a systematic and tamper-proof verification layer within the FurrButler portal, enabling NGOs, NPOs, and CSR partners to independently register and track stray dog vaccinations, chipping, and associated expenses transparently.

---

### 🧩 **Workflow to Implement:**

#### 🐕 **Step 1: Stray Registration (Initiated by NGO/NPO)**

NGOs initiate registration by uploading:

* **Photo:** Clear picture of the stray dog, clearly showing the collar and visible tag.
* **Location Tagging:** GPS coordinates auto-captured or manually set.
* **Stray Details:** Description including breed type, gender, age estimation, fur color, and distinctive marks.

#### 💉 **Step 2: Vaccination Entry (Mandatory Verification)**

Each vaccination entry must include:

* **Vaccination Photo:** Image clearly showing vaccination being administered (auto-watermark "Captured via FurrButler" optional).
* **Vaccination Certificate:** PDF or image upload.
* **Vaccine Information:** Name of vaccine, batch number, and expiration date.
* **Vaccinator Details:** Name and contact of the vet (registered FurrButler vet or external vet with credentials).
* **Digital Signature:** Verified via a temporary digital token or permanent ID provided upon NGO registration, securely linked to the vaccinator's identity.

#### 🧠 **Step 3: Internal Asynchronous Verification Layer**

Implement an internal audit process:

* **Random Audit Sampling:** AI-supported checks to detect reused or manipulated images.
* **Pattern Analysis:** Identify suspicious vaccination entries (duplicate images, high-volume anomalies).
* **Citizen Reporting:** Mechanism for pet parents or citizens to report anomalies directly through the app.

#### 🔖 **Step 4: Unique Identification (UID) Generation**

On successful verification:

* Generate a unique Stray ID.
* Assign collar tag with UID and QR Code.
* Publish data immediately to user-facing tracker.

#### 📈 **Step 5: Expense Tracking**

Transparent financial breakdown for each stray:

* Vaccination cost divided per stray.
* Additional individual expenses tracked (travel, food, accommodation).
* Real-time expense visibility linked to each stray’s profile.

#### 🗺️ **Step 6: Community Interaction and Collar Tagging**

Encourage NGOs to:

* Upload video snippets of collar tagging.
* Allow community comments or updates.
* Color-code collars clearly indicating stray temperament (Green: Friendly, Yellow: Cautious, Red: Aggressive).

---

### 🔧 **Technical Implementation Guidelines:**

* **Frontend:** Simple, mobile-friendly interface for NGOs.
* **Backend:** Secure database (SQL preferred) storing all records, linked clearly to UIDs.
* **Security:** Robust digital signature system, AI image-verification integrations.
* **Transparency:** Real-time expense ledger displayed publicly per stray.

---

### 🧑‍💻 **Testing & Validation:**

Implement initial test data sets and conduct:

* End-to-end verification tests (Registration → Vaccination → UID generation).
* Randomized AI-image-validation trials.
* Expense tracking accuracy checks.

---

### 🚨 **Additional Features:**

* Notification alerts for suspicious activity or audit flags.
* Easy-to-use citizen reporting functionality.

---

Ensure that each component is modular, easily maintainable, and scalable for future integrations (e.g., smart feeders, community interaction zones).

**End Goal:**
A robust, transparent, and user-friendly stray tracking system that ensures autonomy for NGOs while maintaining accountability and public trust.
