Data Processing Agreement

Between FurrButler (Processor) and Vendor (Controller)

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the service agreement between FurrButler ("Processor") and the Vendor ("Controller") for the provision of the FurrButler ERP platform and related services.

Article 1Parties

Data Processor: FurrButler, a pet services technology platform providing ERP, marketplace, and management tools for pet service providers.

Data Controller: The Vendor, being the registered business entity using the FurrButler platform to manage their pet services operations and customer relationships.

Article 2Subject Matter and Duration

This DPA governs the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with the provision of the FurrButler platform services. This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the service agreement between the parties and shall survive termination to the extent necessary for the completion of data processing obligations.

Article 3Nature and Purpose of Processing

The Processor processes personal data for the following purposes:

  • Providing the ERP platform for managing bookings, inventory, and customer relationships
  • Facilitating communication between the Controller and their customers
  • Processing payments and generating financial reports
  • Providing analytics and business intelligence
  • Technical support and system maintenance

Article 4Type of Personal Data

The following categories of personal data are processed:

  • Customer names and contact information (email, phone, address)
  • Pet information (name, breed, species, medical conditions)
  • Booking and service history
  • Payment and transaction records
  • Communication records (chat messages, reviews)
  • Employee data (for vendors with staff management enabled)

Article 5Categories of Data Subjects

  • Pet owners / customers of the Controller
  • Employees and staff of the Controller
  • Sub-contractors and service providers of the Controller

Article 6Obligations of the Processor (FurrButler)

The Processor shall:

  1. Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller
  2. Ensure that persons authorised to process personal data are bound by confidentiality obligations
  3. Implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures
  4. Not engage another processor without prior written authorisation from the Controller
  5. Assist the Controller in responding to data subject requests
  6. Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with data protection obligations
  7. Delete or return all personal data upon termination of services, unless retention is required by law
  8. Make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits
  9. Notify the Controller without undue delay (within 48 hours) upon becoming aware of a personal data breach

Article 7Obligations of the Controller (Vendor)

The Controller shall:

  1. Ensure that the processing of personal data is lawful and that appropriate consents have been obtained
  2. Provide clear and documented instructions regarding data processing
  3. Respond to data subject requests within 30 days
  4. Notify affected data subjects and the supervisory authority of data breaches in accordance with applicable law
  5. Maintain records of processing activities

Article 8Sub-processors

The Processor currently uses the following sub-processors:

  • Replit: Cloud hosting and infrastructure
  • SendGrid: Email delivery services (when activated)
  • Razorpay: Payment processing (when activated)

The Controller will be notified of any changes to sub-processors with 30 days' advance notice.

Article 9International Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the European Economic Area or India, the Processor shall ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, including standard contractual clauses approved by the European Commission or adequacy decisions.

Article 10Security Measures

The Processor implements the following security measures:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS/SSL) and at rest
  • Role-based access control with principle of least privilege
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
  • Access logging and monitoring
  • Incident response procedures
  • Employee security awareness training

Article 11Audit Rights

The Controller has the right to audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA. Audits shall be conducted with reasonable advance notice (minimum 30 days) and shall not unreasonably interfere with the Processor's business operations. The Controller shall bear the costs of any audit.

Article 12Deletion on Termination

Upon termination of the service agreement, the Processor shall, at the Controller's choice, delete or return all personal data within 90 days. The Processor may retain data where required by applicable law, in which case the Processor shall inform the Controller of the legal basis and duration of retention.

Article 13Governing Law

This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India. Any disputes arising from this DPA shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Kerala, India.
