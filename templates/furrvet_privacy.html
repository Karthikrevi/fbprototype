🏥 FurrVet Medical Data Privacy Notice

Special category data processing notice

Important: FurrButler processes veterinary medical records as a data processor on behalf of your clinic. Medical data is processed under GDPR Article 9 with explicit patient consent. You remain the data controller for all patient records.

What Medical Data Is Collected

  • Patient (animal) identification and details
  • Medical history and diagnoses
  • Treatment records and prescriptions
  • Vaccination records
  • Laboratory test results
  • Hospitalisation records
  • Billing and insurance information

Legal Basis for Processing

Medical data is processed under GDPR Article 9(2)(h) — processing necessary for medical diagnosis, the provision of health treatment, and the management of health systems and services. This applies to veterinary medical data by analogy under applicable data protection frameworks.

Data Retention

Medical records are retained for a minimum of 10 years as required by medical legal obligations. This data cannot be deleted on request during the mandatory retention period due to legal retention obligations.

Who Has Access to Records

  • The veterinary clinic staff who created the records (data controller)
  • FurrButler as data processor (for technical hosting only)
  • The pet owner, upon verified request to the clinic
  • Regulatory authorities where required by law

Patient Rights

Pet owners have the right to access their pet's medical records through their veterinary clinic. However, the following exceptions apply:

  • Medical records cannot be erased during the mandatory 10-year retention period
  • Rectification requests must be verified by the treating veterinarian
  • Data portability is available — records can be transferred to another clinic
